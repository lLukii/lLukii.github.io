A cute problem to kickstart 2026
December 25th, 2025
Contributors: Lucas Jiao
Let \(\mathcal{S}\) denote the set of all subsets of \(\{1,2,\dots,2026\}\). Find the arithmetic mean of the largest element across all sets in \(s \in \mathcal{S}\).

Solution: Let's first find the sum of the largest elements \(S\). If we fix the largest element of the subset to be \(k\), then there are \(2^{k-1}\) ways we can choose the remaining elements. Thus, our desired sum turns out to be $$S=\sum_{k=1}^{2026}k\cdot 2^{k-1}$$ and we want to find \(\frac{S}{2^{2026}}\). Now, suppose that $$A(x)=\sum_{k=1}^{2026}x^k=\frac{x^{2027}-x}{x-1}$$ by the geometric series formula. Taking the power rule of the sum, it turns out that \(A'(2)=S\). Differentiating the summation gives us $$A'(x)=\frac{2026x^{2027}-2027x^{2026}+1}{(x-1)^2}$$ so our answer is $$\frac{A'(2)}{2^{2026}}=\frac{2026\cdot 2^{2027}-2027\cdot 2^{2026}+1}{2^{2026}}=2026\cdot 2-2027+\frac{1}{2^{2026}}$$ $$=\boxed{2025+\frac{1}{2^{2026}}}$$ Kinda interesting how that \(2^{-2026}\) term is just floating there.
